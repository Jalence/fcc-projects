Sustainable Sushi

Sustainable sushi is made from fished or farmed sources that can be effectively maintained or can be aquired without harming the ecosystem and environment. Growing concerns for environmental responsibility, economic and social stability, and human health has led to the shift of using more sustainable sushi in restaraunts across the world.

What makes sustainable sushi, sustainable?

There are four main factors that help determine sustainability:

  • Traceability, knowing specifically where and how the seafood was caught or farmed.
  • Overfishing has led certain fish populations to be in severe decline. By allowing the fish species repopulate, we can enjoy them for many generations.
  • Sustainable fishing methods do not damage ocean ecosystems and minimizes or eliminates bycatch (unintended fish or other sea creatures caught during fishing).
  • Responsible and ethical farming practices include providing quality feed, being antibiotic-free and without hormones added, and taking great care not to disturb the surrounding environment.

Sushi to Avoid

Avoid these fish as they are being overfished to unsustainable levels and/or farmed in ways that harm the environment.

  • Bluefin and Yellowfin Tuna (Maguro)
  • Farm Raised Atlantic Salmon (Sake)
  • Freshwater Eel (Unagi)
  • Yellowtail/Japanese Skipjack (Hamachi)

Sushi to Eat

Sushi in this list is being fished and farmed responsibly.

  • Arctic Char (Iwana)
  • Sablefish (Gindara)
  • Salmon Roe (Ikura)
  • Striped Bass (Suzuki)
  • Mackerel (Saba)

But the fish to avoid is also the most popular sushi fish!

Unfortunately that is the reality. If you are unable to find out where the fish came from, it has been most likely harvested in an unsustainable manner. Responsible and ethical sushi establishments will have popular sushi fish that have been sourced sustainably and proudly describe where they source their fish for their restaraunt.

These lists are by no means singular and definitive. The fishing industry is fast changing, new sustainable fishing methods are being introduced, allowing us to enjoy the various flavors of the ocean.

For a more current and updated list of sustainable fish to enjoy, check out the Seafood Watch Program.
